Happy Head for Women: The Overlooked Half of the Catalog

3 min read · By The Crown Report Research Desk · reader-supported, partner links marked (details)

Nearly every hair loss review site writes for men, but roughly 40% of women experience hair loss by 65, and the treatment rules for women are genuinely different, which is why Happy Head's women's line is built the way it is. Here's what most affiliates skip.

Why women's formulas are split by age

The dividing line is pregnancy safety. DHT-blockers like finasteride and dutasteride can cause birth defects and are off-limits for women who are or may become pregnant; even handling crushed tablets is warned against. So Happy Head structures its women's products around that line: women under 50 get formulas built on pregnancy-conscious dosing, like a bundle pairing a 6% minoxidil topical with a low-dose 1.25% oral minoxidil, while women 50+ can use the stronger unisex formulas, including the ProBlend cream with dutasteride, finasteride, and 8% minoxidil (~$79/month).

The spironolactone option

For women, the hormonal lever isn't finasteride, it's often spironolactone, an anti-androgen prescribed off-label for female pattern hair loss. Happy Head offers it as an OralRx at $199 for a four-month supply (about $50/month), with the same dermatologist-consult model as the men's line: online questionnaire, licensed dermatologist review, monthly shipping, and the refund-of-your-latest-month guarantee within 6 months.

Our take

Happy Head is one of the few telehealth services with a genuinely differentiated women's protocol rather than a pink version of the men's page, and having dermatologists gate the prescriptions matters more for women, where ruling out thyroid, iron, and hormonal causes should come first. The same value caveat applies as the men's line: you're paying specialist prices (full scoring in our main review). If cost is the constraint, plain 2 to 5% minoxidil remains the evidence-backed budget start for women.
