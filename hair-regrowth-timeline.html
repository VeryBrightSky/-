Why the dip is the point

That month-2 density drop in the simulator is real and it's called minoxidil shedding: the drug pushes resting follicles into a new growth cycle, so old hairs fall out first to make room. It feels like the treatment is failing. It's actually the treatment working. The people who make it past this window are the ones who see results; the people who quit here never do. Everything past month 3 in the tool is the payoff for holding through the dip.

The rule that matters: judge treatment at 6 months, not before, and take month-0 and month-6 photos in the same light. Memory is a terrible measuring tool for something this gradual.

Want to shorten the wait?

You can't skip the biology, but stronger formulas can move the curve. Dutasteride tends to show thickening slightly earlier than finasteride, and higher-concentration minoxidil pushes more follicles into growth (the trade-offs are in our dutasteride vs finasteride and 8% minoxidil guides).
