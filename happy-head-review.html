What Happy Head sells

Happy Head is a dermatologist-founded telehealth company built around one idea: put the proven hair loss drugs, finasteride, dutasteride, and minoxidil, into custom-compounded topical formulas so you treat the scalp instead of the whole body. You fill out a medical questionnaire online, a licensed dermatologist reviews it, and if you're a candidate they prescribe a formula shipped monthly.

The flagship products are the TopicalRx solutions. The standard version combines 0.25% finasteride with 8% minoxidil, and the advanced formulas add dutasteride, the stronger DHT blocker. That 8% minoxidil number matters: over-the-counter Rogaine tops out at 5%, so this is a higher concentration than anything you can buy off a shelf. They also sell oral versions (finasteride from about $24/month, dutasteride around $59), a SuperCapsule that combines dutasteride, minoxidil, and vitamin D in one pill (~$71/month), plus women's formulas, shampoo, and supplements.

Nobody should pretend this is cheap. Generic oral finasteride from a competitor can cost a third of Happy Head's topical. You're paying for three things: the custom compounding, the higher minoxidil concentration, and a dermatologist (not a general NP) reviewing your case. Whether that's worth it depends entirely on why you want a topical in the first place, which brings us to the real question.

The case for topical: side effects

Oral finasteride works and is FDA-approved, but a small percentage of men experience sexual side effects like reduced libido or erectile dysfunction, and that risk is the main reason men quit or never start. Topical finasteride is compounded and prescribed off-label, meaning it's not FDA-approved in topical form, but research so far suggests it delivers the drug to the scalp with much lower blood-level absorption, which may lower the side effect risk. That trade (less systemic exposure in exchange for a higher price and a daily routine) is Happy Head's entire pitch. Our topical finasteride guide covers the evidence in detail.

Set expectations: every legitimate treatment, Happy Head included, takes 3-6 months to show visible results, and a temporary shedding phase in the first weeks is normal with minoxidil. Anyone promising regrowth in 30 days is lying to you.

Pros & cons at a glance

Pros

  • Strongest legal topical combos we've reviewed: up to 8% minoxidil (OTC caps at 5%) plus finasteride or dutasteride
  • Licensed dermatologists, not general telehealth providers, review every Rx case
  • Clear per-product pricing published on every page, rare in this category
  • Refund of your most recent month within 6 months; pause or cancel anytime; free shipping
  • Topical route may reduce systemic exposure vs the pill

Cons

  • Premium pricing: flagship topicals ~$71 to $79/mo vs ~$25 to $35/mo for generic basics
  • Topical finasteride/dutasteride is compounded and off-label, with a younger evidence base than the pill
  • No FSA/HSA payment (Hims accepts it)
  • Daily measured application takes more discipline than a pill

What we don't love

Price is the obvious one. The refund policy covers your most recent month's supply within 6 months, which is fair but not the "full money-back guarantee" some blogs describe it as. Unlike Hims, Happy Head doesn't take FSA/HSA payments. And topical application is a real commitment: a measured 1ml on the scalp daily, which some people abandon faster than swallowing a pill.

Verdict: who should buy it

Get Happy Head if you want the strongest available topical combination, you're specifically avoiding oral finasteride because of side effect concerns, and $70 to $80/month fits your budget. Skip it if you just want the proven basics at the lowest price; plain oral finasteride plus store-brand 5% minoxidil does the job for most men at a fraction of the cost, and we compare that route in Happy Head vs Keeps.

Happy Head FAQ

How much does Happy Head cost per month?
Oral finasteride starts around $24/month. The flagship custom topicals with finasteride or dutasteride plus 8% minoxidil run roughly $71 to $79/month on subscription ($89 to $99 one-time), and the full catalog spans about $22 to $179/month depending on the formula.
Do you need a prescription for Happy Head?
Yes, for the Rx products. You complete an online dermatology questionnaire, a licensed dermatologist reviews it, and prescribes a formula if you're a candidate. OTC items like shampoo and supplements don't require it.
Is topical finasteride FDA approved?
No. Oral finasteride (1mg) is FDA-approved for male pattern hair loss; topical finasteride is a compounded, off-label formulation prescribed at a provider's discretion. Research so far suggests it produces much lower blood levels of the drug, which may reduce side-effect risk. Details in our topical finasteride guide.
