How to Cancel Happy Head, and When Not To

3 min read · By The Crown Report Research Desk · reader-supported, partner links marked (details)

Quick answer: Happy Head lets you pause or cancel your subscription anytime through your account, and the refund policy covers your most recent month's supply within 6 months if you are unsatisfied. Cancel before your next billing date to avoid another shipment. No phone tree required.

The steps

Log into your Happy Head account, open your subscription settings, and choose pause or cancel. Pausing is worth considering if you are stopping for travel or budget reasons rather than quitting, because restarting later means a fresh consult otherwise. If you want money back on your latest shipment, contact their support within the 6 month window and request the refund of your most recent month, which is what their guarantee actually covers. Keep the confirmation email.

One honest warning about timing: subscriptions bill on a cycle, and cancelling the day after a charge means that shipment is on its way. Check your next billing date in the account page first and cancel ahead of it.

Before you cancel because "it is not working"

If you are inside your first 8 weeks and shedding more than before, that is the documented shedding phase, not failure. It is the single most common reason people quit right before the treatment starts visibly paying off. Our timeline tool and the shed checker can tell you whether what you are seeing is on script. If you are at month 6+ with matched photos showing nothing, that is a real signal, and stopping is reasonable.

Also worth knowing: stopping any DHT blocker or minoxidil means the clock restarts. Gains fade over the following months and loss resumes its old pace. That is true of every product in this category, not just this one.

Cheaper instead of cancelled

If price is the reason you are leaving, you have middle options before quitting treatment entirely. Happy Head's own oral finasteride runs about $24 a month, a third of the flagship topical. Or step down to the generic route we map in Happy Head vs Keeps: oral finasteride plus store brand 5% minoxidil covers the proven basics for roughly $25 to $35 a month. Staying on something cheap beats stopping something expensive.
