Happy Head vs Keeps: Specialist Strength vs Budget Basics

3 min read · Two telehealth hair loss services with opposite philosophies. Here's the honest split.

Reader-supported · partner links marked · how we’re funded

Happy HeadVSKeeps

Keeps built its business on one insight: the two FDA-approved treatments for male pattern hair loss, oral finasteride and topical minoxidil, are cheap generics, so sell them cheap. Happy Head went the other direction: compound the same drugs (plus dutasteride) into stronger, customized topical formulas prescribed by dermatologists, and charge for the craftsmanship. Neither is wrong. They're for different people.

When Keeps is the right call

If you're early in your hair loss, have no problem taking a daily pill, and want the proven, FDA-approved protocol at the lowest sustainable price, Keeps wins on pure value. Hair loss treatment is forever, you stop, you lose the gains, so the monthly price you can tolerate for years matters more than any premium feature. The boring answer (generic finasteride + 5% minoxidil) has decades of evidence behind it.

When Happy Head is worth the premium

Three situations flip the verdict. First, if you've read about oral finasteride's sexual side effects and know you won't stay consistent on the pill, a topical route you'll actually use beats a cheaper pill you quit. Second, if you've already run the basics for a year without results, Happy Head's dutasteride formulas and 8% minoxidil are the legal next step up in strength. Third, if you want an actual dermatologist making the call rather than a generic telehealth provider. Full breakdown in our Happy Head review.
