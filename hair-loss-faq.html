Hair Loss FAQ, Answered With Numbers

Updated July 2026 · By The Crown Report Research Desk · reader-supported, partner links marked (details)

Straight answers to the questions we get most, with links to the full numbers. Every answer reflects pricing and formulations verified July 2026.

How long does hair loss treatment take to work?
Every legitimate treatment, from generic minoxidil to premium compounded topicals, shows first visible results in 3 to 6 months, with full results near 12. A temporary increase in shedding during weeks 2 to 8 on minoxidil is normal and expected.
What is the strongest minoxidil you can get?
Over the counter minoxidil tops out at 5%. Compounded prescription versions go higher; Happy Head compounds up to 8%, the strongest we have reviewed. Higher concentration can help after a plateau on 5% but carries more irritation risk.
Is dutasteride better than finasteride for hair loss?
Dutasteride suppresses roughly 90% of serum DHT versus about 70% for finasteride, and beat finasteride 1mg on hair counts in a 917 man randomized trial. Finasteride remains the usual first choice because it is FDA approved for hair loss and clears the body in days rather than months.
Does topical finasteride have fewer side effects than the pill?
Research so far shows topical finasteride produces much lower blood levels than oral dosing, which may reduce the risk of sexual side effects. The topical form is compounded and off label, so its evidence base is younger than the pill's decades of data.
How much does Happy Head cost per month?
Happy Head's catalog runs about $22 to $179 per month. Oral finasteride starts near $24, and the flagship compounded topicals combining finasteride or dutasteride with 8% minoxidil run about $71 to $79 on subscription.
What is the cheapest hair loss treatment that actually works?
Store brand 5% minoxidil at roughly $10 to $15 a month is the proven entry point. Adding generic oral finasteride brings the total to about $25 to $35 a month and covers both proven mechanisms, which is the setup we recommend for most men.
Why is my hair shedding more after starting minoxidil?
That is the shedding phase: minoxidil pushes resting follicles back into growth, and the old hairs they were holding fall out first, typically in weeks 2 to 8. It settles by month 3 or 4 and is a sign the treatment is engaging, not failing.
Can women use finasteride or Happy Head?
Happy Head offers women's formulas built around minoxidil dosing by age group and spironolactone rather than standard finasteride, which is not used in women who are or may become pregnant. Pregnancy safety rules shape every women's formula in this category.
