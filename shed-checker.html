Why shedding happens on treatment

Minoxidil pushes resting follicles back into their growth cycle, and the old hairs sitting in those follicles get evicted first. That is the shed: a real increase in fallen hairs, usually starting in weeks 2 to 8 and settling by month 3 or 4. It looks exactly like the treatment failing, which is why so many people quit at the precise moment it starts working. The regrowth timeline shows where it sits in the full 12 month arc.

Not every shed is that shed. Patchy loss, a shed that will not end after month 4, or shedding paired with scalp symptoms or a recent illness points at other causes, from alopecia areata to telogen effluvium after stress, and those deserve a professional's eyes rather than a longer wait.
