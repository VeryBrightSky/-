8% Minoxidil vs 5%: What Stronger Really Buys You

3 min read · By The Crown Report Research Desk

Reader-supported · partner links marked · how we’re funded

Is 8% minoxidil better than 5%? Sometimes. 5% is the proven, FDA approved over-the-counter standard and the right starting point. 8% is a compounded prescription strength worth considering only after 6 to 12 months of consistent 5% use has plateaued, and it carries more irritation risk.

Walk into any pharmacy and the strongest minoxidil you can buy is 5%, the FDA-approved over-the-counter ceiling that's been the standard since the 90s. But compounding pharmacies, working under a prescription, can formulate higher: 6%, 8%, and beyond, usually blended with finasteride or dutasteride. The question is whether stronger means better or just more expensive.

What the evidence really shows

5% is the concentration with the mountain of evidence: in the frequently cited research, roughly 4 out of 5 men saw meaningful regrowth benefit with consistent use. Above 5%, the data thins out. The logic for higher concentrations is straightforward, more drug reaching the follicle, and some studies of higher-strength formulations report stronger response, particularly in men who plateaued on 5%. But this is a smaller, younger evidence base, and higher concentrations also raise the odds of the classic minoxidil complaints: scalp irritation, itching, and unwanted hair on the face or neck where the solution migrates.

Who should care about 8%

If you haven't tried 5% for at least 6 to 12 months, start there; you may never need more, and you'll save serious money (run the numbers in our cost calculator). The 8% conversation is for two groups: men who used 5% consistently and stalled, and men who want finasteride or dutasteride delivered topically anyway, since the compounded combo puts everything in one daily application. In the US, the best-known source of 8% combinations is Happy Head, whose dermatologists prescribe it blended with finasteride or dutasteride (~$71 to 79/month, reviewed here).
