Dutasteride vs Finasteride: What the Trials Actually Show

3 min read · By The Crown Report Research Desk · reader-supported, partner links marked (details)

Which is stronger? Dutasteride. It blocks both forms of the DHT-producing enzyme, cuts serum DHT about 90% versus finasteride's roughly 70%, and beat finasteride 1mg on hair counts in a 917-man randomized trial. Finasteride remains the standard first choice because it is FDA approved for hair loss and clears the body in hours, not months.

Both drugs attack the same target: DHT, the hormone that shrinks genetically sensitive hair follicles. The difference is coverage. Finasteride blocks the type II form of the 5-alpha-reductase enzyme; dutasteride blocks type I and type II. In published research, that translates to serum DHT reductions of roughly 90% for dutasteride versus about 70% for finasteride.

What the head-to-head evidence says

The largest direct comparison randomized 917 men with androgenetic alopecia to dutasteride, finasteride 1mg, or placebo. At 24 weeks, dutasteride 0.5mg produced significantly greater hair count and growth improvements than finasteride. Smaller randomized studies and a 2024 systematic review point the same direction: deeper DHT suppression, more regrowth. Reported side-effect rates in trials were broadly similar between the two drugs, though dutasteride's five-week half-life means anything you experience takes far longer to wash out.

So why isn't everyone on dutasteride?

Three reasons. It's off-label for hair loss in the US, so many providers default to the approved option. Its long half-life makes side effects, and conception planning, a bigger commitment. And for a large share of men, finasteride is simply enough. The sensible ladder most dermatologists follow: start with finasteride (or a topical), reassess at 12 months, escalate to dutasteride if loss is aggressive or progress stalls.
