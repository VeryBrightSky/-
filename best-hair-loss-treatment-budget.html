The Best Hair Loss Treatment for Your Budget

4 min read · By The Crown Report Research Desk · reader-supported, partner links marked (details)

The short version: at $10 to $15 a month, store brand 5% minoxidil is the proven start. Around $25 to $35, generic oral finasteride plus that same minoxidil covers both jobs and is what most men should run. At $70 to $80, Happy Head's compounded topicals buy maximum legal strength and a dermatologist, worth it mainly if pill side effects are your blocker. More money buys strength and convenience, not speed: every tier takes 3 to 6 months to show.

Tier 1: the $10 to $15 starter

One product: 5% minoxidil, store brand, applied daily. It is the only regrowth treatment you can buy without a prescription that has FDA approval behind it, and the generic works identically to the famous bottle at a third of the price. What it does not do is address the hormone driving the loss, so think of this tier as slowing the bleeding rather than fixing the leak. Fine for very early thinning, or for testing whether you will actually stick to a daily routine before spending more. The 8% vs 5% guide covers what stronger versions add later.

Tier 2: the $25 to $35 workhorse

This is the tier we point most men to, and we say that as a site whose biggest commissions come from the tier above it. Generic oral finasteride, about $10 to $25 a month through services like Keeps or your own doctor, blocks the DHT that shrinks follicles. Pair it with the Tier 1 minoxidil and you are running both proven mechanisms, defense and regrowth, for roughly the cost of two streaming subscriptions. The full math and who this fits is in Happy Head vs Keeps.

The honest catch at this tier is the pill itself: a small percentage of men get sexual side effects on oral finasteride, and that risk is the single biggest reason people either avoid this tier or move up from it. Our side effects guide covers the real numbers.

Tier 3: the $70 to $80 specialist

Happy Head's compounded topicals put finasteride or dutasteride and 8% minoxidil into one daily scalp application, prescribed and monitored by dermatologists. You are paying for three things you cannot get downstairs: concentrations above the shelf ceiling, the topical route's lower systemic absorption, and a specialist reviewing your case. If the pill's side effect profile is what has kept you untreated, this tier exists for you, and it earned our Editor's Choice for custom topicals. If you tolerate the pill fine, Tier 2 does the core job cheaper. The complete scoring is in the full review.

Choosing in one minute

Budget under $20: Tier 1 today, add finasteride when you can. Comfortable at $30 and fine with a daily pill: Tier 2, the default answer. Avoiding the pill specifically, or plateaued after a year on the basics: Tier 3. Still unsure where you land: the 60 second quiz asks five questions and routes you, and the cost calculator shows what each path totals over 1 to 5 years, because every tier is a subscription measured in years, not bottles.
