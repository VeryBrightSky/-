Happy Head vs Hims: The Specialist vs The Superstore

3 min read · Both prescribe finasteride and minoxidil online. The differences are concentration, focus, and price.

Reader-supported · partner links marked · how we’re funded

Happy HeadVSHims

Hims is the biggest name in men's telehealth, hair loss is one aisle in a store that also sells ED meds, skincare, and mental health care. Its hair plans run roughly $15 to $60 per month, it accepts FSA/HSA payment, and it offers finasteride and minoxidil in convenient formats including sprays and chews. Happy Head does exactly one thing: dermatologist-prescribed hair loss formulas, compounded stronger than anyone mainstream, at $71 to $79/month for its flagship topicals.

Where Hims wins

Price and friction. If you're new to treatment, a ~$20 to $30/month Hims plan with a chewable or spray is the lowest-effort way to start the proven drugs, and paying with pre-tax FSA/HSA dollars effectively discounts it another 20-30% for many people. For a first-year protocol on a budget, that's hard to argue with.

Where Happy Head wins

Ceiling. Hims sells the standard doses; Happy Head compounds above them: 8% minoxidil versus the 5% OTC ceiling, plus dutasteride, the stronger DHT blocker, in both pill and topical form. If standard treatment hasn't delivered, or you want a dermatologist specifically managing a topical-only protocol to minimize systemic exposure, Happy Head is the stronger tool.
